# spectral_synchrony

The hidden harmony of a complex system of oscillators as they spontaneously synchronize.

## Concept

This artwork explores the Kuramoto model, a mathematical model used to describe synchronization in large populations of coupled oscillators. It visualizes the transition from chaos to order, as individual "sparks" of activity begin to influence their neighbors, eventually forming a rhythmic, breathing web of light.

## Technique

- **Kuramoto Model Simulation**: 200 oscillators with unique natural frequencies interact via a coupling strength parameter. Their phases evolve over time based on the average phase of their spatial neighbors.
- **Coherence-Based Rendering**: Connections between oscillators are drawn only when their phases become coherent (aligned) or anti-coherent. The brightness and color of these connections reflect the degree of synchronization.
- **Dynamic Pulsing**: Each node pulses in luminosity according to its internal phase, creating a collective "heartbeat" as the system synchronizes.
- **SCREEN Blending**: Layers of additive light create a glowing, high-contrast aesthetic against a deep charcoal background.

## Visual Impression

A dark field of flickering sparks that slowly begin to pulse in unison; glowing electric-cyan and deep-magenta connections bridge the sparks, forming a rhythmic, breathing network that feels alive and interconnected.
