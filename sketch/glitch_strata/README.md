# glitch_strata

A generative artwork simulating digital decay and the "archaeology" of corrupted data layers.

## Concept
The piece imagines a vertical cross-section of a digital storage medium that has undergone extreme data corruption. It combines the geological concept of "strata" with the modern aesthetic of "glitch art," exploring the beauty found in systematic error and memory decay.

## Technique
- **Recursive Stratification**: The canvas is subdivided into horizontal layers of varying heights, each assigned a unique "corruption profile."
- **Horizontal Tearing**: 1D Perlin noise controls horizontal displacement of pixel rows, creating the characteristic "tearing" seen in analog and digital signal failure.
- **Block Corruption**: Random regions are overwritten with solid color blocks or noise, simulating localized sector failure.
- **Cyber Palette**: High-contrast CMYK-inspired neon tones (mint, magenta, cyan) are set against industrial grey and charcoal.
- **Post-Process Scanlines**: A global scanline overlay and occasional vertical "data spikes" complete the CRT/digital display aesthetic.
