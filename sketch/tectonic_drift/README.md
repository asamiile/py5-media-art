# tectonic_drift

A generative artwork simulating the macroscopic movement and collision of planetary tectonic plates.

## Concept
The piece captures the vast, slow-motion drama of planetary evolution. It visualizes the shifting of massive crustal plates, the building of volcanic mountain ranges at convergent boundaries, and the formation of glowing magmatic rifts where the earth is being pulled apart.

## Technique
- **Dynamic Voronoi Tessellation**: The planetary crust is partitioned into massive plates using Voronoi seeds that drift according to simulated geological velocities.
- **Boundary Analysis**: Vector math is used to determine whether plate boundaries are convergent (colliding) or divergent (separating) based on relative velocity and direction.
- **Voluminous Mountain Building**: Convergent zones are rendered with multiple layers of jagged, fractal-noise lines to simulate the massive scale of mountain ranges.
- **Magmatic Rift Synthesis**: Divergent zones feature a multi-step additive glow to represent molten magma surfacing from the planet's mantle.
- **Craton & Surface Texture**: Plates include internal "cratons" (ancient geological cores) and a stochastic grain to simulate rocky basalt and granite surfaces.
