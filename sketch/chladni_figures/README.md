# chladni_figures

**Theme**: "Resonance geometry" — Chladni figures reveal the hidden geometry of sound; sand on a vibrating plate settles at the nodal lines where the plate is still

**Technique**: Superposed vibrational modes (standing wave eigenfunctions of a square plate), threshold coloring at nodal zones

**Description**: Five vibrational modes of a square plate are superimposed: f(x,y) = Σ aᵢ·[sin(mπx)·sin(nπy) + sin(nπx)·sin(mπy)]. The symmetric sum of each (m,n) pair preserves 4-fold plate symmetry. Pixels where |f| < threshold mark the nodal lines where a real plate is stationary — and where sand physically accumulates. A smooth transition zone (sand → gold → dark plate) mimics the real density gradient at the node boundary.

**Palette**:
- Plate surface: `#120e0a` dark warm felt
- Nodal zone (sparse sand): `#b49450` dull gold
- Nodal peak (dense sand): `#e4dab0` pale sand-cream
