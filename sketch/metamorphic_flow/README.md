# metamorphic_flow

A generative artwork simulating the look of metamorphic rock strata using domain-warped fractal noise.

## Concept
The piece explores the concept of "deep time" and the immense geological pressure required to fold and compress rock layers into intricate, swirling patterns. It evokes the appearance of polished agate or gneiss strata.

## Technique
- **Domain Warping**: Multi-octave fractal noise is used to warp the coordinate space of a primary noise field, creating organic, "folded" patterns.
- **Geological Palette**: A curated gradient from deep obsidian through slate and sienna to pyrite gold and quartz white.
- **Mineral Inclusions**: High-frequency noise thresholds simulate thin mineral veins and crystalline inclusions.
- **Micro-Texture**: Stochastic grain is applied to the final pixel field to mimic the porous and crystalline surface of real stone.
