# phyllotaxis

**Theme**: "Nature's arithmetic" — the golden angle (≈137.508°) as the universal packing optimum found in sunflowers, pinecones, and nautilus shells

**Technique**: Vogel's phyllotaxis formula (θ=n·137.508°, r=c·√n), 8500 seeds, color and size encoded by radial position

**Description**: 8500 seed discs are placed using Vogel's formula — each successive seed rotates by the golden angle and expands its radius by √n. This irrational angle prevents any two seeds from ever aligning radially, achieving the densest possible packing. Seeds are drawn back-to-front (outermost first) so the dense center overlaps the sparse edge. Radial position encodes age (center = first seed placed) and maps to the amber→gold→cream color gradient; seed size decreases outward to simulate perspective. The dual spiral families of Fibonacci numbers (34 and 55 arms) emerge as an optical effect.

**Palette**:
- Background: `#080603` near-black warm
- Center (oldest seeds): `#b45f19` amber
- Middle zone: `#e1af3c` golden yellow
- Outer rim (newest): `#f0e1b9` pale warm cream
