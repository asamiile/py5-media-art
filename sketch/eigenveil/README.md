# eigenveil

Hidden order surfaces through a dark woven field.

## Concept

The work imagines structure as something almost textile: not a diagram of a network, but the pressure pattern left by a system trying to organize itself under the surface.

## Technique

- **Spectral Graph Modes**: A jittered lattice is converted into a weighted graph, then Laplacian eigenvectors become low-frequency structural fields.
- **Bilinear Field Expansion**: Small eigenmode grids are expanded to canvas scale, retaining broad mathematical order without showing the original lattice directly.
- **Quantized Contour Threads**: The spectral field is sliced into fine luminous bands that read as woven filaments.
- **Stress Accents**: Local field gradients add restrained amber highlights where the hidden structure bends most sharply.

## Visual Impression

A quiet, full-canvas veil of teal and violet threads floats over near-black, with amber tension points revealing the hidden geometry underneath.
