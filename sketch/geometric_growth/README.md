# geometric_growth

A generative artwork exploring recursive mathematical growth and structural complexity.

## Concept
The piece visualizes the intersection of organic growth patterns and rigid geometric subdivision. It portrays multiple L-system structures—reminiscent of both coral reefs and high-tech circuit boards—growing across a deep obsidian field and anchored by architectural subdivision tiles.

## Technique
- **L-System Branching**: Multiple L-system structures are seeded across the canvas, using a recursive grammar to generate complex, self-similar forms.
- **Recursive Geometric Subdivision**: A secondary logic performs local Mondrian-style subdivisions into smaller tiles at terminal nodes and across the background.
- **Multi-Layered Depth**: The composition is built in three layers: large, low-opacity background tiles for depth; the primary L-system structures; and finally, high-detail foreground subdivisions.
- **Premium Material Palette**: The artwork uses a curated palette of obsidian, gold leaf, and architectural white, creating a luxurious and high-tech aesthetic.
- **Organic Jitter**: Small amounts of stochastic noise are introduced into the branching angles and segment lengths to ensure the structures feel alive rather than purely mechanical.
