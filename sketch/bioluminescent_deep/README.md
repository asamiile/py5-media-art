# bioluminescent_deep

A generative artwork exploring the "alien" beauty of deep-sea bioluminescent life.

## Concept
The piece visualizes the midnight zone of the ocean, where life creates its own light in the absolute darkness. It portrays several procedural creatures—reminiscent of jellyfish and polyps—pulsing with ethereal phosphorescence and surrounded by a cloud of bioluminescent plankton and marine snow.

## Technique
- **Procedural Organic Shapes**: Creatures are generated using a hybrid of radial noise and organic subdivision, creating non-repeating, soft-body forms.
- **Internal Glow Organs**: Each creature features internal glowing structures rendered with layered, semi-transparent circles to simulate complex biological anatomy.
- **Curling Tentacle Simulation**: Long, flowing tentacles are generated using a noise-driven walk combined with a sinusoidal curling force, creating natural, underwater movement.
- **Organic Plankton Clustering**: Bioluminescent micro-particles are clustered around the main creatures using a distance-based probability model to simulate a living ecosystem.
- **Marine Snow Accumulation**: The scene is grounded by a dense field of pale "marine snow" particles, providing depth and a sense of volume to the deep-sea void.
- **ADD Blend Luminousity**: Extensive use of additive blending allows the bioluminescent colors (magenta, cyan, and green) to overlap and create intense, vibrant highlights.
